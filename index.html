Martins Aquarium of Rare and Exotic Fish

Tank Cleaning Tips

  • If your aquarium is newly established and has not been cycled yet, you need to test the water to determine if it has 0 ppm ammonia, 0 ppm nitrites, and less than 40 ppm nitrates.
  • If your aquarium is already cycled, then the goal is to keep nitrate levels below 40 ppm.
  • In order to maintain a clear view of our fish, scrub the tank walls with an algae scraper.
  • If algae has grown on the lid, you can easily rinse it off in the sink.
  • If you keep live aquarium plants, take this time to remove any dead leaves and trim down overgrown foliage.
  • Before removing any water, make sure to turn off or unplug all equipment.
  • At least once a month, you should clean the filter.
  • At this point, you can finally refill the tank with clean water.
